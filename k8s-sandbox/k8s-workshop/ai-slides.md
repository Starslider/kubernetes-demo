- "Write me a story about a cat"

## What Does Generative AI Create?

- Text (articles, emails, code)

- Video (animations, scenes)

**Examples you know:**

## How It Works (Simplified)

**Three Steps:**

1. **Training:** Learn from billions of examples
   - Read millions of books and websites
   - Study millions of images

2. **Pattern Recognition:** Build "knowledge" of patterns
   - What words go together?
   - What makes good content?

3. **Generation:** Create new content following patterns
   - Not copying‚Äîcreating something new
   - Following learned "rules"

**Not magic:** Very sophisticated pattern matching at massive scale

## Section 2
### Understanding Large Language Models

---

## What is an LLM?

**LLM = Large Language Model**

**"Large" means:**
- Trained on enormous amounts of text
- Billions of parameters (adjustable values)
- Requires massive computing power

**"Language Model" means:**
- Focused specifically on human language
- Understands and generates text
- Can translate, summarize, answer questions

**LLMs are a type of Generative AI specialized for language**

---

## Popular LLMs

**GPT-4** (OpenAI)
- Powers ChatGPT
- Most well-known

**Claude** (Anthropic)
- Longer conversations
- You're seeing it now in Claude Code!

**Gemini** (Google)
- Multimodal (text + images)

**LLaMA** (Meta)
- Open-source foundation

---

## How LLMs Generate Text

**Think of it like autocomplete on steroids:**

You type: "The cat sat on the..."

**Simple autocomplete:** "mat"

**LLM:** Predicts next words based on:
- Context of entire conversation
- Learned patterns from training
- Your specific question

**Result:** Can write paragraphs, articles, or code

**Key:** Predicting likely words, not "thinking"

---

## What LLMs Excel At

**Great for:**
- Answering questions
- Summarizing documents
- Writing content (emails, reports)
- Translation between languages
- Explaining complex topics
- Brainstorming ideas
- Writing and debugging code

**Current Limits:**
- Can make up facts confidently
- Math/logic errors possible
- Knowledge cutoff dates
- No true understanding

## Section 3
### What is Agentic AI?

---

## The Key Difference

**LLM (Conversational AI):**
- You: "Find me a restaurant for Friday"
- LLM: "I'd recommend checking OpenTable. Consider Italian restaurants..."
- **You still do all the work**

**Agent (Agentic AI):**
- You: "Find me a restaurant for Friday"
- Agent: *Checks your calendar ‚Üí Searches restaurants ‚Üí Makes reservation ‚Üí Adds to calendar*
- **Agent does the work**

**The shift:** From assistant that talks ‚Üí assistant that acts

---

## What is an AI Agent?

**An AI system that can:**

1. ‚úÖ Understand a goal
2. üß† Plan steps to achieve it
3. üõ†Ô∏è Take actions autonomously
4. üìù Learn from results
5. üîÑ Adapt its approach

**Key ingredient:** **Agency** = ability to act independently

**Analogy:**
- LLM = Expert consultant (gives advice)
- Agent = Employee (does the work)

---

## How Agents Work

**The Agent Loop:**

1. **Observe:** What's the current situation?
2. **Think:** What should I do next?
3. **Act:** Execute an action (use tools/APIs)
4. **Reflect:** Did that work?
5. **Repeat:** Until goal is achieved

**This is "agentic behavior":** Acting autonomously toward goals

---

## Agent Capabilities

**Agents can:**

**Use Tools:**
- Call APIs and web services
- Search databases
- Send emails/messages
- Run calculations

**Plan & Reason:**
- Break down complex problems
- Chain thoughts together
- Adapt when plans fail

**Remember:**
- Learn from past interactions
- Remember your preferences

| | Generative AI | LLMs | Agentic AI |
|---|---|---|---|
| **What it does** | Creates content | Converses | Takes actions |

## How They Relate

**Think of it as layers:**

üî∫ **Top: Agentic AI**
- Uses LLMs + tools + planning
- Takes autonomous actions

üî∫ **Middle: LLMs**
- Specialized for language
- Powers conversations

üî∫ **Bottom: Generative AI**
- Foundation technology
- Creates content

**They build on each other:** Agents use LLMs, which are a type of Generative AI

---

## When to Use What?

**Use Generative AI when:**
- Need single creative output
- No iteration needed

**Use LLMs when:**
- Need explanations or advice
- Conversational refinement helps
- No actions required

**Use Agentic AI when:**
- Have a goal, not just a question
- Multiple steps needed
- Want autonomous execution

## The Hallucination Challenge

**What are hallucinations?**
- AI confidently stating false information
- Making up facts or citations

**Why it happens:**
- Predicts likely text, not truth
- No built-in fact-checking

**Solution:**
- Give agents tools to verify facts
- Require citations
- Human review of critical outputs
- Don't trust blindly!

---

## Safety & Control

**The Human-in-the-Loop Approach:**

**Low-risk tasks:**
‚Üí Full autonomy (fast)

**Medium-risk tasks:**
‚Üí Human review before execution (balanced)

**High-risk tasks:**
‚Üí Human decision-making (safe)

**Best practice:** Right balance of speed and safety for your use case

## Near Future (2025-2027)

- More reliable tool use
